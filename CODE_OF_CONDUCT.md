# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful and considerate**: Treat others as you would want to be treated
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and help them learn
- **Be collaborative**: Work together to solve problems and build the best possible solution
- **Be constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Be professional**: Maintain a professional and friendly demeanor
- **Be patient**: Remember that everyone has different levels of experience and expertise

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual harassment, or bullying
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language, jokes, or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Personal attacks**: Insults, ad hominem attacks, or personal criticism
- **Spam**: Unwanted or off-topic content, repeated messages
- **Trolling**: Deliberately provocative or off-topic messages
- **Threats**: Threats of violence or any form of intimidation
- **Privacy violations**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Disruptive behavior**: Actions that disrupt the community or project progress

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all SwapNet community spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Official communication channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)
- Community events and meetups
- Private communications when related to project work

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers:

- **Email**: conduct@swapnet.dev
- **GitHub**: Create a private issue or contact maintainers directly

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

### Consequences

Project maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including but not limited to:

- **Warning**: Private or public warning about inappropriate behavior
- **Temporary suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Content removal**: Removal of inappropriate content
- **Legal action**: Involvement of law enforcement if necessary

### Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly accused or punished:

1. Submit an appeal to conduct@skillbridge.dev
2. Include a detailed explanation of the situation
3. Provide any relevant evidence or context
4. The appeal will be reviewed by different maintainers
5. A decision will be made within 7 business days
